PROOF OF HUMAN WORK — FOUNDATION GRANTS

Supporting research, development, and community initiatives

GRANT PROGRAMS

Research Grants

The Foundation supports research initiatives that advance the PoHW protocol and related technologies.

Research Areas:

  • Post-quantum cryptography for PoHW
  • Privacy-preserving verification methods
  • Protocol durability and long-term viability
  • Zero-knowledge proof implementations
  • Federated registry architectures
  • Cross-chain anchoring mechanisms

Grant Amounts:

  • Small grants: $5,000 - $15,000
  • Medium grants: $15,000 - $50,000
  • Large grants: $50,000+ (by special consideration)

Community Grants

Supporting developers, educators, and community builders working on PoHW-related projects.

Supported Activities:

  • Developer tooling and SDKs
  • Documentation and educational materials
  • Integration projects (Word, Notion, etc.)
  • Community events and workshops
  • Open source contributions

Grant Amounts:

  • Micro grants: $500 - $2,000
  • Small grants: $2,000 - $10,000
  • Medium grants: $10,000 - $25,000

APPLICATION PROCESS

Step 1: Submit Proposal

Submit your grant proposal via email to: grants@proofofhumanwork.org

Required Information:

  • Project title and description
  • Objectives and expected outcomes
  • Timeline and milestones
  • Budget breakdown
  • Team qualifications
  • How the project advances PoHW

Step 2: Review Process

  • Initial review (2-3 weeks)
  • Technical evaluation by advisory committee
  • Ethics review (if applicable)
  • Board approval for large grants

Step 3: Award & Reporting

  • Grant agreement signed
  • Milestone-based disbursements
  • Progress reports required
  • Final report and deliverables

ELIGIBILITY

Who Can Apply:

  • Individual researchers and developers
  • Academic institutions
  • Non-profit organizations
  • Open source projects
  • Community groups

Requirements:

  • Project must advance PoHW protocol or ecosystem
  • Results must be open source (where applicable)
  • Compliance with Foundation ethics guidelines
  • Transparency in reporting

PAST GRANTS

Grant recipients and their projects will be publicly disclosed (with recipient consent) to maintain transparency.

PROOF OF HUMAN WORK — CODE OF ETHICS

Ethical principles and standards

CODE OF ETHICS

Status: Draft — Pending formal adoption

A PDF version will be available upon formal adoption by the Ethics Review Board.

1. Privacy and Anonymity

  • Privacy is a fundamental right, not a privilege
  • Protocol must enable pseudonymous verification
  • No requirement for identity disclosure
  • Minimal data collection principles

2. Accessibility

  • Verification must be accessible to all
  • No gatekeeping or paywalling of truth
  • Low-friction participation
  • Resource-constrained context support

3. Openness

  • Open source by default
  • Open standards and specifications
  • Open participation in governance
  • No proprietary dependencies

4. Durability

  • Commitment to long-term viability
  • Backward compatibility preservation
  • Institutional independence
  • Century-scale planning

5. Non-Discrimination

  • Equal access regardless of geography
  • No discrimination based on identity
  • Language and cultural inclusivity
  • Economic accessibility

6. Transparency

  • Public documentation of decisions
  • Cryptographically verifiable actions
  • Open governance processes
  • Financial transparency

7. Ethical Use

  • Protocol must not enable surveillance
  • No use for censorship or oppression
  • Respect for human rights
  • Ethical AI assistance disclosure

8. Accountability

  • Foundation accountable to community
  • Dispute resolution mechanisms
  • Ethics Review Board oversight
  • Community feedback integration

ENFORCEMENT

Ethics Review Board

The Ethics Review Board is responsible for:

  • Reviewing ethical violations
  • Investigating complaints
  • Recommending corrective actions
  • Ensuring compliance with code

Reporting Violations

To report an ethical violation:

  • Email: ethics@proofofhumanwork.org
  • All reports are confidential
  • Investigation process is transparent
  • Outcomes are publicly disclosed
